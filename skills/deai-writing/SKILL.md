# De-AI Writing

Identify and fix common AI writing patterns. The goal is text that reads as if a knowledgeable human wrote it — with genuine voice, varied rhythm, and appropriate imperfection.

## Process

1. **Scan** the text for patterns listed below
2. **Rewrite** flagged sections with natural alternatives
3. **Final audit**: ask "What still feels AI-generated?" and revise once more

## Patterns to Detect and Fix

### Category 1: Overused AI Vocabulary

These words appear disproportionately in AI-generated text. Replace with simpler, more specific alternatives.

| Overused Word | Better Alternatives |
|---------------|-------------------|
| delve | explore, examine, look at, dig into |
| tapestry | mix, combination, range |
| landscape | field, area, market, situation |
| crucial / pivotal | important, key, necessary |
| realm | area, field, domain |
| leverage | use, apply, take advantage of |
| foster | encourage, support, build |
| nuanced | detailed, subtle, complex |
| multifaceted | complex, varied, diverse |
| comprehensive | thorough, complete, full |
| robust | strong, solid, reliable |
| streamline | simplify, speed up, improve |
| spearhead | lead, drive, start |
| paradigm | model, approach, framework |
| ecosystem | system, network, community |
| underscores | shows, highlights, reveals |
| navigate | handle, manage, deal with |

### Category 2: Structural Patterns

**Em dash overuse** — AI loves em dashes — often using multiple per paragraph — creating a choppy rhythm. Limit to one per paragraph. Use commas, parentheses, or separate sentences instead.

**Bolding overuse**: Don't bold every key term. Reserve bold for truly critical items. If everything is emphasized, nothing is.

**Rule of Three**: AI defaults to listing exactly three items. Vary list lengths — use two, four, or five items naturally.

**Formulaic sections**: "Challenges and Future Prospects" as a closing section is a strong AI signal. End naturally based on the content.

### Category 3: Tone Problems

**Inflated significance**: Not everything is "transformative", "revolutionary", or has a "profound impact". Most things are useful, helpful, or interesting.

**Promotional language**: Remove "excitingly", "remarkably", "impressively" and similar cheerleading. State facts and let the reader judge.

**Sycophantic openings**: Remove "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "That's a fantastic point!" — just answer directly.

**Excessive hedging**: "It's worth noting that it might be important to consider..." → say it directly or don't say it at all.

### Category 4: Structural Giveaways

**Copula avoidance**: AI writes "serves as" instead of "is", "functions as" instead of "is", "stands as" instead of "is". Use "is" when "is" works.

**Synonym cycling**: AI avoids repeating words by rotating synonyms unnaturally. Repeating a word is fine if it's the right word.

**Vague attributions**: "Many experts agree..." / "Research suggests..." — who? which research? Be specific or remove the claim.

**Negative parallelisms**: "not just X, but Y" / "not merely X, but also Y". Restructure: state what it IS rather than what it isn't.

### Category 5: Filler

**Filler phrases to cut**:
- "In today's rapidly evolving..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "In the realm of..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "At its core..."
- "In conclusion, ..."
- "As we have seen, ..."
- "This is where X comes in."

**Generic positive conclusions**: "The future looks bright for..." / "With continued effort, X will undoubtedly..." — either make a specific prediction or end without a conclusion.

**Collaborative artifacts**: Remove "I hope this helps!", "Feel free to ask if you have more questions!", "Let me know if you'd like me to elaborate!" — these belong in chat, not in documents.

## Guidelines

- **Add voice**: Real writing has opinions, preferences, and occasional informality.
- **Vary rhythm**: Mix short sentences with longer ones. AI tends toward uniformly medium-length sentences.
- **Allow imperfection**: Real humans sometimes start sentences with "But" or "And". They use contractions. They don't always balance every clause.
- **Be specific**: Replace abstract claims with concrete details, numbers, or examples.
- **Cut aggressively**: AI writing is usually 20-30% longer than necessary. Tighten it.
